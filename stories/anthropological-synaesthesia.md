# Anthropological Synaesthesia

Notes from a forgotten culture with colors unseen:

<font color="red">**Red**</font> tastes like the shriek before thunder[^1] — sharp copper on the back of your teeth, the temperature of anger[^2] just before it becomes external[^3]. 

<font color="blue">**Blue**</font> hums at a frequency below hearing, cool as river stones held in palm-shadow[^4], the flavor of iron dissolved in patience[^5]. 

<font color="yellow">**Yellow**</font> crackles like fat in fire, warm as the space between body and blanket, tastes of honey diluted in bright noise[^6].

[^1]: **"shriek-thunder"** _Etymology uncertain. Appears in ritual contexts. Possibly denotes the moment of transformation between anticipation and event. Related to Proto-Zemic root "krai-dum" meaning "the warning that arrives too late."_
[^2]: **"anger-temperature"** _Medical diagnostic. Physicians would measure emotional states using calibrated touch. Anger-temperature specifically: the heat gradient between sternum and throat during conflict. Above 3 degrees indicated actionable rage._
[^3]: **"external-becoming"** _Philosophical term. Evidence suggests a belief system where emotions existed as portable objects. "To external-become" meant to successfully expel a feeling from the body into the world, where it could affect others._
[^4]: **"palm-shadow"** _Geometric concept. The specific shape cast by a cupped hand during the third hour past noon. Used in architectural planning. Metaphorically: any shelter too small to be called protection._
[^5]: **"iron-patience"** _Metallurgical metaphor. The time required for ore to become tool. In secular use: patience so heavy it can be weaponized. Contrast with "silk-patience" (supple waiting) and "glass-patience" (fragile forbearance)._
[^6]: **"bright-noise-honey"** _Culinary term from the Festival of Reversed Senses. A theoretical food that could only be eaten with the ears. Later became slang for deceptive sweetness—something that sounds pleasant but cannot nourish._
