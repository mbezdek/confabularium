# The Chain

I tell what I seen. First I is smelling the chicken.

It from two night ago, the bones of it down in the tall silver can, lid press tight the way only thumbs know. Bones in plastic, plastic in can, can shut. Still it come through. Sweet-rot, salt-fat, old heat. It come through all them layers and sit in the room with me and Hendricks and the bad thing already on the way.

Hendricks at the table with the cereal he always eat, the red box with the bird. He pour too much milk. Always too much. It go gray and thin at the bottom and he lift the bowl and drink it off. I seen it three hundred some time. Blue shirt, collar fray left. He look at the little flat bright thing. He think I by the back door for the cool tile on my belly.

That true. The tile cool. Also I watching him.

Also I seen what come at nine.

It come the way come-things come: whole. Like a smell that reach before the thing that make it. Pickett Road. Black car black like hole in drain. Driver look flat bright thing same as Hendricks got now. Hendricks in the road. The before of it — he look at his shoes. Left lace brown where the white one broke. He bend. The car —

Chicken push up strong. A chain too. It pull same as fear pull, same as road pull a car. I hold. I hold cause Hendricks stand now, bowl in sink, and the leaving start.

Here the thought I chew and can't break: if I seen it, it already is. Nine on Pickett a fact same as this tile cold. Clocks just walk on top. What I do a fact too. I in the links. I can't step out.

But.

When fear come off a person, it come before they know. Body first. Mind after. I known this before I known anything — known it the way I know which way the wind come from, which house got the dog that bite, which nights Hendricks cry quiet in the back room. Fear-smell wet in a person before the face show it. Before the hands go wrong. Order to knowing. Maybe a first thing can lean on a next thing. A bend later — car already gone.

Time is the chain. I looking for a link that move.

Hendricks take the brown. I know before I look. I look. Brown.

Clock say 8:50.

By the door the hook. On it the gray jacket, Yolanda. She come some nights, warm touch, find the place behind my ear Hendricks miss. She smell citrus and cold keys. 

I need him see it. The stop. The step round. The look down. The small wonder. Ten second enough. Car and Hendricks near share a breath. Ten might be wider than that.

8:51.

I stand. Legs go before thought end. They do what legs do. I go hook. Sleeve in my teeth. Dust. Old rain. Citrus-metal. I pull. Hook scrape wood. Hanger twist. Weight drop sudden and land between him and door with a soft thud.

Hendricks stop.

He look me. He look gray jacket. I watch the small works of him — heel back, chin down, hand hover — and he not yet himself, or he behind himself, chain got new link, I done it, I —

"Biscuit," he say, soft, the laugh in it. The voice for when I make a wrong he can't mind.

He step round the gray. One step. No reach. No look to left lace. He just go round like water round a stone and the moment seal up and all is brown, brown, black, the door right there.

I did not seen that. Ten second I need and he give none.

He put his hand on me — the flat press that mean later. Warm through my coat. I feel the full of it. I lean in. I take cedar, and salt, and the soap, and under all that, him — just him.

"Be good, aye?"

Door open.

Door close.

Now kitchen and the clock say 8:55 and the tall silver can I can't open and the chicken in it, bones of it, two nights cold inside plastic and metal. I press my nose to the cold side. Want pull one way. Fear pull other. Both chain.

I think ten second. I think if the gray on floor held him at all? Or if every move I make was already back in the links before nine, set there long before me, before Hendricks, before Pickett had a name? I think the black car, head down in the light, the brown lace, the bend.

Cedar at the door fading.

I lift my head and take what left.

I listen for the car on Pickett.

I can't hear it yet.
